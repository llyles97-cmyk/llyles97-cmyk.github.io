About

Lyles Mom

I'm a personalization strategist and audience intelligence analyst based in Philadelphia. I specialize in turning behavioral data into systems that actually influence how organizations make decisions — not just how they report on them.

My path into this work started through Year Up and OpenClassrooms, where I built the foundation that took me from apprentice to full-time employee inside a global enterprise environment. That journey shaped how I work: practical, adaptive, and deeply focused on turning learning into impact.

Over the past several years, I’ve grown from that early-career start into leading global personalization and analytics initiatives across 10+ international markets. My work spans audience segmentation, engagement modeling, experimentation frameworks, and omnichannel measurement — translating complex datasets into strategies that teams can actually execute on.

I focus on a specific problem: the gap between what systems measure and what people actually do. Most reporting frameworks are built for consistency, not accuracy. They flatten behavior into metrics that are easy to track but difficult to act on. I build models, scoring systems, and analytical frameworks that surface those gaps — and turn them into opportunities.

Alongside my professional work, I’m building Cult Circulation — a media and analytics platform exploring how attention moves across culture, platforms, and audiences. It’s a space where I apply audience intelligence thinking to music, media, and digital ecosystems, bridging structured data with real-world behavior.

I’m currently focused on roles in audience intelligence, consumer insights, and measurement strategy across media, streaming, healthcare, and platform-driven companies. I’m especially interested in environments where data is abundant, signals are noisy, and clarity creates competitive advantage.

Outside of work, I spend a lot of time in theaters, studying how stories are structured and received, and thinking about how attention forms, peaks, and disappears — both on screen and in data.

"The gap between what institutions measure and what audiences actually do is not a bug — it is a feature of how measurement systems get built."

I'm currently targeting roles in audience intelligence, consumer insights, and measurement strategy at music, media, healthcare, and corporate analytics organizations. If the role involves finding the signal inside noisy data and translating it into something a stakeholder can act on, I want to hear about it.
